1.1 BACKGROUND AND MOTIVATION
India is one of the top producers of fruits and vegetables in the 
world. But even with such a large harvest, a lot of this food goes 
to waste every year. This happens mainly because there aren’t 
enough good storage facilities after the crops are harvested.
Fruits and vegetables spoil quickly, so they need to be kept in 
cold storage to stay fresh for longer.
Cold storage helps in slowing down spoilage and keeps 
the produce fresh until it reaches the market. However, most of 
the cold storage units in India are old or not built in the right 
way. Many of them don’t consider the local weather or 
environmental conditions. Also, they don’t track important 
things like temperature and humidity in real-time or over a long 
period. Because of this, it becomes hard to build or improve 
cold storage systems that are energy-saving and suitable for 
different regions.
Monitoring temperature, humidity, and other 
environmental factors is very important to make cold storage 
more effective. Without proper data, farmers and storage 
operators cannot know if the storage conditions are right. Poor 
conditions can lead to food spoilage and higher electricity use, 
which increases costs and harms the environment.
So, there is a strong need to collect and use real-time and 
long-term data from cold storage units. This will help in 
designing better systems that save energy and reduce food

1.2 PROBLEM STATEMENT
Conventional cold storage units usually run without any kind 
of system to monitor or respond to environmental changes. In 
most cases, they do not have sensors or tools to check and 
record important conditions such as soil temperature at 
different depths, the temperature of the air around the unit 
(ambient temperature), or the level of moisture in the air 
(relative humidity) over time. Because of this, the storage 
environment often becomes unstable or inconsistent, which can 
cause fruits, vegetables, and other perishable items to spoil 
faster than expected.
When cold storage systems operate without proper 
feedback or real-time monitoring, they may use more 
electricity than necessary. For example, cooling might continue 
even when it's not needed, or it might fail to adjust to sudden 
temperature or humidity changes. This not only increases 
energy costs but also affects the quality and safety of the stored 
produce. Over time, these issues lead to significant losses in 
both food and money.
Another major problem is the lack of data. Without proper 
records of environmental conditions, it becomes very difficult 
to study how different factors affect cold storage performance.
